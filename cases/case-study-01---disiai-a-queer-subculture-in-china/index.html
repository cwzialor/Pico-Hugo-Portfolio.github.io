In the second semester of my Master’s at Shanghai Jiao Tong Univerisity, Shanghai, I independently wrote a paper and which was accepted for presentation at the 110th Annual Convention of the National Communications Association in New Orleans, USA.

My paper, “Disiai: A Queer Subculture in China” explores China’s LGBTQ+ community. In China the LGBTQ+ community navigates a landscape of social norms, legal frameworks, and cultural attitudes different to those of Western countries. Therefore, understanding must considers the unique cultural, philosophical and historical context of Chinese gender identity.

This paper describes the findings of a netnographic study of Chinese subculture ‘Disiai’. Social posts were scraped using wget and pup for content analysis providing insights that bring into question the inclusively LGBTQ+ umbrella in contemporary Western thought.

delves into the evolving dynamics of queer identity in one of the world’s largest and most rapidly changing societies providing insights to the cultural limitations of gender studies research.

My research goals were threefold:

First, through the exploration of cultural, philosophical, and historical factors my paper aims to elucidate cultural and contextual understanding of queer versus heterosexual identity in China. While highlighting importance cultural specificity connecting with China’s LGBTQ+ community.

Second, my paper attempts to challenge Western-centric narratives and moral frameworks of sexuality; and aims to broaden readers perspectives to better equip them to engage in cross-cultural dialogue and collaboration.

Third, by exploring the Disiai community I hope to accurately record and introduce LGBTQ+ subcultures that are unique to China to a broader audience outside of China. To foster new perspectives on the fastest changing society in the world.

Overall, my ambition is that my contribute to cross-cultural gender studies encourages readers and attendees to critically reflect on their own assumptions of the LGBTQ+ umbrella in a global context enabling them to develop a more nuanced understanding of cross-cultural gender studies.
